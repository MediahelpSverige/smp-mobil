Testing | Projects | Producer gas

The project ”Producer gas in glassworks” finished

SMP Glafo (Glass Research Institute) in Växjö and Group glassworks Orrefors Kosta Boda AB have collaborated in research projects on producer gas in glassworks. SMP has now completed its part in the project, meant to provide a Producer gas heating, or carburetor, sufficient to heat a glass furnace. ​

The producer gas plant which SMP has constructed and commissioned at the glassworks in Kosta appeared to work as planned. The plant operated, after trimming, completely automatically. ​

The critical functions fuel input, level control and ash removal has been designed by SMP on an existing carburetor. The difficulty has been to ensure the input of wood pellets and discharging waste products, ie ash, soot and carbon. For the gasifier to operate continuously it was required that the fuel was fed without any air possibly leaking in and disturbing the gasification, and without any toxic gas leaking out. This has been solved by using airtight locks on the input side, and a device that compresses ashes and soot etc. into a compact powder on the discharge side. This compression of the residual product made ​​that no gas could seep out, and no air enters.

In addition, one got a dry powder with a relatively high density as a residue. This product contains a high proportion of energy in the form of carbon, and could either be returned to the process via the fuel feed, or used to manufacture barbeque briquettes or similar.

Producer gasverkets kapacitet visade sig vara tillräcklig både för att hålla en kontinuerlig drifttemperatur i ugnen på 1100 grader och för att höja temperaturen till 1400 grader vid smältning av glas.

When the carburetor's gas flow had tuned, normal work with glass production could be performed at the Producer gas heated furnace. Tests have shown that the molten glass is clean and clear and no visible impurities followed with the gas into the glass furnace.

It requires large amounts of energy to heat glass furnaces in glassworks. Normally, you use electricity or gas to the burners that will keep the temperature in the ovens. It's in the oven as glass is then melted. One of the goals of the project was to investigate whether it is possible to replace these other fuels with wood available in glassworks proximity and using it to produce a gas that can be burned. By using pelletized wood is hoped to reduce the total cost of the energy that glass furnaces require.

Glafo together with Orrefors Kosta Boda and professor Björn Zethraeus at Växjö University has planned the project. The project was funded by the Swedish Energy Agency and Orrefors Kosta Boda.

The producer gas technology advances

For SMP, the project was a natural continuation of previous projects.             A Producer gas assembly that SMP used in a previous EU Projects (see P & F No. 3 -2001), has been modified to be used in the project.

Modification and adaptation and test drive of the gas heater was conducted at SMP's Testing Laboratory in Umeå.             When the system had been tested out in Umeå it was transported to Kosta where it was mounted up, and now have supplied gas sufficient to heat the glass kiln to 1400 degrees Celisus.

Spontaneously combustible gas

The producer gas unit would produce gas that is so energetic and hot as possible. This is because the burner that is at the glass furnace requires a gas temperature of about 500 degrees C as it leaves the gasifier.                  Producer gas is led into the glass furnace and ignite when mixed with air.

The entire facility was controlled and monitored by the advanced control and monitoring systems available at Orrefors, Kosta and Boda glassworks.

Reliability is everything

For practical reasons, it was a requirement that the gas plant could produce gas continuously for several days without interruption. One of the main tasks of the SMP during the modification phase was therefore to provide a reliable system.

This meant that the plant was modified in several respects. Among other things, it was required that the ash produced during combustion could continuously be ejected during operation without producer gas leaking out the same way. Another requirement was that the fuel feed functioned smoothly even if the moisture content of the raw material varied slightly.

Pellets instead of wood chips

Some important features distinguish wood pellets from wood chips. The pellets are denser than chips and also a more homogeneous material. Therefore, it is easier to transport than wood pellets. Although the risk of fuel will be trapped in the unit decreases.

Hazardous gas!

Producer gas contains carbon monoxide (CO) and is therefore dangerous. Personal safety is very important at all Producer gas production. Anyone in the vicinity therefore bears a gas alarm that warns acoustically on gas would leak out.

As an additional protection there was also a stationary monitoring system for air CO level to be mounted adjacent to the gas heater.
