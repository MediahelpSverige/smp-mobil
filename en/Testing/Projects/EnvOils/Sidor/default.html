Environmentally friendly oils - are they dangerous?

The issues surrounding environmentally friendly oils are always current.         Reports from Finland has attracted attention because they indicated that environmentally friendly oils could affect human health adversely. ​

Click here to download the report "Health and environmental impact of environmentally friendly oils."

SMPs literature study

In order to gain more knowledge on the subject, SMP has done a literature study funded by SLO Fund at the Royal Institute of Agriculture and Forestry Academy.

The study has included a review of newspaper articles and reports published in Finland.

following (Swedish) articles and studies were the basis of the results compiled by SMP:

  • Artikel i Skogsteknik, nr 3, 2002.
  • Artikel i Skogsteknik, nr 2, 2001.
  • Slutrapport Biooljeprojekt, Uleåborgs universitet, Finland. 2001.
  • Slutrapport från Arbetshygien i Kuopio, Finland. 2002
  • Finsk artikel i TEHO nr 4 1998.
  • IVL Rapport B-1267, Miljöfarlighetsbedömning av hydrauloljor för arbets- och
  • skogsmaskiner, 1997.

    • Summarized results

    In this study conducted at the Occupational Health in Kuopio, it appears that              forest machine operators suffered from hand eczema and other work-related skin symptoms more than the peer group, but they could not show any association with              use of bio-oils.

    The reported symptoms were mild and did not interfere work performance or leisure.              It mentions several other risk factors such as work load, atopic eczema, sensitive skin, the wrong method for blending stump treatment solution and almost significance for contact with oil/hydraulic hose several times per week. It clarifies that contact with oils increases the risk of skin symptoms, regardless of oil type.

    The interview that was conducted by METLA, Oulu gives a completely different picture.              It has about 70% of those using bio-oils indicated that they have skin problems              caused by bio-oils. Similarly indicates more than 70% of those using biological oils that differently had technical problems with the environmental oils.

    It is not reasonable that two different sub-search solutions will give so completely different results. The search for accession made ​​by the Occupational Health is more thorough, more comprehensive and scientifically conducted. We have here also taken into account other influencing factors and compared with a control group.

    Questionnaires designed it in METLA's study can easily become guiding, and the result is misleading. The study gives, however, a clear signal of the respondents' attitudes towards the use of bio-oils.
