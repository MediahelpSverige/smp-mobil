Testing | Projects | Fuel for chainsaws

Research on the reference fuel's impact on exhaust emissions from chainsaws/h1>

SMP in Umeå implemented in 2004-2005 a project with comparative measurements of exhaust emissions from chainsaws and brush cutters. The comparison was made between emissions from the reference fuel used for exhaust certification of engines, and regular fuels normally used when working with machinery. The project was funded by the Royal Academy of Agriculture and Forestry funds, SLO Fund. ​

Click here to download the report "Comparative emission measurements of two-stroke fuel".

Summarized results

As part of improving the health and environment for Europeans, a new Directive (97/68EG) came in force in 2004, such as includes chain saws, brush cutters, grass trimmers and leaf blowers.

Problemet är nu att denna referensbensin inte finns att tillgå på den öppna marknaden och kan alltså inte används i praktiskt arbete. Det som används i praktiken är i stället: The directive and it's amendment directive 2002/88/EC requirements specified limits and how the emission measurements to be carried out. Here is also stated what kind of reference fuel to be used for the measurements.

  • regular gasoline (for cars) where the machine owner himself takes care of oil mixing
  • s.c. alkylate where the user manages oil mixing
  • s.c. oil mixed alkylate (mixed with 2% fully synthetic two stroke oil)

    • This means that the machines in practical applications are running with a fuel that does not meet the fuel used in the approval. To find out if the requirements are met boundaries even with the market fuels, comparative measurements was carried out in December 2004 by SMP in Umeå and was financed by funds from the SLO Fund.

    The goal of the project was to verify possible differences in emissions between the Directive's unique reference fuel and the fuels normally used in the Swedish market and to establish whether the different fuels complying with the Directive limits and to recognize the work environmental impacts.

    The survey was conducted with the following three gasoline grades:

  • Reference gasoline under Directive 2002/88/EC, annex 5
  • Environmentally friendly two-stroke fuel (alkylate trading as Aspen)
  • Trade Petrol, so-called unleaded 95 octane gasoline

    • The comparative measurements were carried out on two pieces stroke engines, a chainsaw engine Husqvarna 346XP and a brush cutter engine Stihl FS 450K.

    The results of the comparative tests were briefly as follows: Leaving aside the nitrogen oxides, have the reference fuel have the highest values ​​measured in this fuel comparison. This involves using a fuel that does not benefit the engine manufacturer at emission testing as the basis for approval of the directive. If the engine meets the requirements of the reference fuel, it will also make it with those on the Swedish market commonly used fuels.

    When it comes to health and environmental concerns, there is a clear environmental benefit of using alkylate. Many employers have discovered this and those who work professionally with hand held power tools are already using this type of fuel.
