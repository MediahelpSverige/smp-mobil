Testing | Projects | Properties of bicycle helmets

Properties of bicycle helmets in addition to the CE marking

SMP has conducted a project about the properties of bicycle helmets with the support of the research foundation of the state insurance companies. ​

Click here here to download the report "Properties of bicycle helmets".

Background

Today it is a problem that the use of bicycle helmets is generally low and decreasing in the younger groups. Severe head injuries and fatalities among cyclists would reduce if the use of bicycle helmets was at a higher level.

A way to put the helmet issue to focus is to follow the same model as for crash tests and bike tests, to design a system where aspects other than the legal requirements for bicycle helmets are assessed by impartial test entries.

In order to be allowed to sell bicycle helmets, which is considered as personal protective equipment, they should meet the European safety requirements, which the manufacturer shows by attaching the CE mark on the helmet. However, there are significant differences betwwen CE-marked helmets, for example in terms of adjustments and how well the adjustment is retained after a period of use. These aspects are also important to safety. A helmet that requires daily adjustments will not be used properly and there are accidents where one has been able to prove that the damage was caused because the helmet was improperly adjusted.

Much work are currently put to, including some by the state insurance companies, inform the importance of adjusting the helmet properly. This work would be facilitated if there were test results showing which helmets are the safest ones regarding not only the requirements for CE marking.

Researched criterias

A system which rates the helmets may particular take into account if:

  • Adjustment straps are easy to adjust and lock
  • Adjustment straps retain these properties after long term use
  • Adaptation of the helmet to individual head shapes can be done easily and the settings remain in use

    • It is important that an evaluation system:

  • Is optional
  • Being designed by the leading helmet suppliers so that it is marketed by the suppliers themselves
  • Based on objective repeatable measurements/tests
  • Not so costly that it will lead to large-price competitive disadvantages for those who choose to join

    • Project conclusions

    The project was divided into two phases: phase 1 was regarded as a preliminary study.

    Since the preliminary study showed that there was an interest in a evaluation system from the helmet suppliers the project went on into phase 2. In phase 2 test methods related to the preliminary criteria set was developed.

    After evaluation of various methods, it was found that it was not possible to obtain the repeatability required for the methods to be used for the objective evaluation of properties.

    In early May 2006, a seminar took place where providers, Agencies and other stakeholders took part of the project results and discussed the future development needs in terms of, for example, the helmet strap system.
